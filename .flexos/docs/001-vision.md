# Vision

## The One-Line
A gamified language learning app that teaches real-world slang, idioms, and internet culture through interactive meme translation.

## The Problem
Traditional language apps (like Duolingo or Babbel) teach textbook vocabulary optimized for surviving as a tourist ("The apple is red", "Where is the library?"). This leaves learners functionally illiterate when faced with real-world internet culture, group chats, slang, and memes. Furthermore, tools like Google Translate fail to capture cultural context, double entendres, puns, and the specific humor inherent in native social media content, often spitting out confusing literal translations.

## The Insight
Humor and internet culture are the ultimate tests of fluency. If you can understand a culture's memes, you understand their language and their mindset. By replacing sterile textbook sentences with viral, user-generated content, we can weaponize the addictive, dopamine-driven scroll of social media for genuine cultural education.

## Who It's For
**Primary Persona: The 11pm Scroller (Intermediate Learner)**
They have a 400-day streak on a traditional language app but still can't understand foreign TikTok comments. They know the grammar, but lack the cultural vocabulary. They want to participate in internet culture, not just order a train ticket.

**Secondary Persona: The Expat/Immigrant**
Living in a new country, they can handle professional settings but feel alienated in casual group chats because they don't understand the local meme formats or hyper-specific regional slang.

**Explicitly NOT For:**
- Absolute beginners learning the alphabet or basic phonetics.
- People studying for formal academic exams (DELE, JLPT, TOEFL).
- Children (content is native internet culture, which includes profanity and mature themes).

## What It Is
Lingo.jpg is a dark-mode, TikTok-style vertical feed where every post is a curated meme in the user's target language with obscured text. Users must decipher the punchline or slang to earn "Glot Points". Upon guessing, an AI provides a snappy "Culture Breakdown" explaining the joke, the literal translation, and the cultural context. Points are spent in a "Vault" to unlock localized digital stickers. Saved memes become a contextual spaced-repetition flashcard deck.

## What It Is Not
- **Not a grammar textbook:** We will never explain verb conjugation tables or subjunctive rules.
- **Not a structured course:** There are no "Units", "Levels", or linear progression paths. It is an endless feed.
- **Not an audio app:** Explicitly excluding voice recognition, pronunciation grading, or listening comprehension tests for v1.
- **Not a generic flashcard app:** No isolated words without context. Every vocabulary word must be tied to a specific meme.

## Why Now
LLMs (specifically models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet) are finally capable of parsing cultural nuance, regional slang, and complex humor accurately, solving the "Google Translate" gap. Simultaneously, internet culture is more globalized than ever. Learners are fatigued by sterile, gamified curriculums and crave authentic, native connection.

## What Success Looks Like
- **3-Month (Launch):** 
  - 5,000 active users completing at least 15 meme translations per day.
  - 40% Day-1 Retention; 15% Day-30 Retention.
  - High engagement on the "Vault" unlock loop (users spending >80% of earned points).
- **12-Month (Scale):** 
  - 100,000 Monthly Active Users.
  - UGC "Field Notes" generating 80% of the new daily curriculum, creating a self-sustaining content flywheel.
  - Viral acquisition loops driven by users exporting Vault stickers to iMessage/WhatsApp.

## Core Values Embedded in the Product
1. **Cultural context over grammatical perfection:** Slang, profanity, and internet shorthand are valid vocabulary.
2. **Learning should feel like scrolling:** The UI must be frictionless, dark, immersive, and fast. Zero loading spinners between memes.
3. **Explain jokes without ruining them:** The AI voice must be internet-native, irreverent, and concise. No academic lecturing.
4. **Status is the best reward:** Gamification must lead to tangible social flexing (custom emojis, public streaks) rather than arbitrary badges.
